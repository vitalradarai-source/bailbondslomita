LASD Lomita Station: Where Lomita Arrests Are Processed

After an arrest in Lomita, the defendant is transported to the LASD Lomita Sheriff Station, located at 26123 Narbonne Ave, Lomita, CA 90717 (phone: 310-539-1661). The Lomita Station is a full-service LASD patrol and holding facility. Its jurisdiction covers the City of Lomita, Rancho Palos Verdes, Rolling Hills Estates, Rolling Hills, and portions of unincorporated LA County in the South Bay.

The Lomita Station holds defendants for short-term pre-arraignment custody. For defendants facing serious felony charges, or those requiring longer-term detention, transfer to a central LASD facility is standard. The most common receiving facilities are the Pitchess Detention Center (29320 The Old Road, Castaic) and Men's Central Jail (441 Bauchet St, Los Angeles).

The LASD Inmate Information Center at app5.lasd.org/iic is the official public tool for locating someone in LA County custody. Records typically populate 2 to 6 hours after booking is completed at the station. The LASD bail information line is (213) 473-6080.

The Booking Timeline at LASD Lomita Station

Booking is a mandatory administrative process that must be fully completed before bail is set and a bond can be posted. Here is the sequence at the Lomita Station:

Step 1
Arrest and Transport
The arresting LASD deputy transports the defendant to 26123 Narbonne Ave. The arrest report is completed — capturing charges, identifying information, and arrest circumstances.
Step 2
Personal Property Inventory
All personal items are inventoried, bagged, and secured. A property receipt is issued to the defendant. This is typically when phone contact with the arrested person is severed.
Step 3
Live Scan Fingerprinting
Electronic fingerprints are submitted to state (Cal-DOJ) and federal (FBI) databases. This step confirms identity and surfaces any outstanding arrest warrants from other California counties or federal agencies. Warrants discovered here can delay or complicate release even after bail is posted.
Step 4
Booking Photo
A photograph is taken and linked to the case record. Once the full booking record is entered into the LASD database, it becomes visible through the public inmate locator.
Step 5
Medical Screening
California law mandates a health screening for every person booked into a jail. The screening addresses injuries, medications, chronic conditions, and mental health. Any medical issue identified here can add time to the overall process.
Step 6
Bail Amount Set
The bail amount is assigned per the 2026 LA County Felony or Misdemeanor Bail Schedule, issued by the Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles. For most charges, the schedule amount is set at booking without judicial involvement. Certain serious offenses require a magistrate review before bail is established.
Step 7
Housing or Transfer
Defendants held at Lomita Station may post bail directly there. Those transferred to a central facility must have the bond posted at the receiving location. Total time from arrest to bail eligibility at a transferred facility is typically 8 to 16 hours for felony cases.

2026 LA County Bail Schedule — South Bay Edition

The Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles issues annual Felony and Misdemeanor Bail Schedules, effective January 1. These set the standard bail for each charge classification at booking. A judge at arraignment — required within 48 hours under California Penal Code § 825 (excluding Sundays and court holidays) — may increase or reduce bail based on the defendant's specific circumstances.

Here are representative bail amounts from the 2026 LA County bail schedule, reflecting charges commonly seen in the South Bay corridor:

The window before arraignment is your best opportunity to secure release at the scheduled bail amount. If arraignment occurs without bail posted on a serious charge, the judge may increase it — particularly if the defendant has a prior record or the DA argues for elevated bail.

How a Bail Bond Works in Lomita

Under California Insurance Code § 1800.4, the standard bail bond premium is 10% of the total bail amount. For a $30,000 bail, you pay $3,000 to the bondsman — not $30,000 to the court. The bondsman posts the full bail as a surety bond with the court, guaranteeing the defendant's court appearances. The 10% premium is a non-refundable service fee — it is earned when the bond is posted.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is someone taken after an arrest in Lomita?

Lomita arrests go to the LASD Lomita Sheriff Station at 26123 Narbonne Ave, Lomita, CA 90717. Defendants facing serious felony charges or requiring extended custody are transferred to central LASD facilities such as Pitchess Detention Center (Castaic) or Men's Central Jail (Los Angeles).

How long does booking at Lomita Station take?

Misdemeanor bookings at Lomita Station typically take 4 to 8 hours from arrest to bail eligibility. Felony cases with transfer to a county facility should expect 8 to 16 hours total from arrest to bond posting at the receiving facility.

What does a bail bond cost in Lomita?

The standard California bail bond premium is 10% of the total bail amount. For a $50,000 bail, that's $5,000 upfront — not $50,000. Angels Bail Bonds offers payment plans for qualifying clients who cannot cover the full premium immediately.

Can bail be posted at LASD Lomita Station directly?

Yes. All LASD facilities accept bail bonds 24 hours a day. If the defendant is transferred to a central facility, the bond is posted there. Angels Bail Bonds monitors all transfers and handles bond posting at whichever facility is holding the defendant.

Is the bail bond premium refundable if my case is resolved quickly?

No. The 10% premium is the bondsman's non-refundable service fee for posting the surety bond. Case outcome does not affect the premium. It is earned at the moment the bond is posted with the court.
